üìå What is a Derivative?

A derivative of a function at a point represents the slope of the curve at that point.

For straight lines, slope is constant. For curves, the slope changes ‚Äî so we calculate it at a specific point.

üìê Slope Concept

The slope of a line is defined as:

Slope = (Change in y) / (Change in x)

To find the slope of a curve, we:

  • Take two nearby points on the curve
  • Draw a secant line
  • Bring the points closer
  • The secant becomes a tangent
