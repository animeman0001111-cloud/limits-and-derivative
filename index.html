WHAT IS A LIMIT:-

A limit describes the behavior of a function as the input value x gets closer to a particular number, without necessarily being equal to that number. It tells us what value the function f(x) is approaching as ùë• approaches a given point from both the left and the right. Even if the function is not defined at that point, or has a different value there, the limit may still exist. Limits form the foundation of calculus and are essential for understanding concepts such as continuity, derivatives, and integrals, as they help explain how functions behave near specific points and at infinity..
